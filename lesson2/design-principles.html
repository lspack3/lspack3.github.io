Design Principles

Principle 1: Contrast

Odd Pears

Odd Pears Website

Odd Pears is a website that specializes in selling unique and very colorful socks. Their website is a great example of contrast in design. The salmon pink tags on the dark blue banner stand out on the white background. Not to mention all the other bright colors and fun designs on the socks that just pop. The eye can't help but be drawn to all the action happening with the contrast on this site!

Principle 2: Repetition

Kohl's

Kohl's Website

Kohl's is a popular department store to Americans. Thier site is a great example of the use of repetition in web design. The left panel is the perfect spot to see repetition. Here you can see the same font, color, size, and icons all repeated. It demonstrates order. You can even see repetition being used near the top of the page with the category images.

Principle 3: The Rule of Thirds

Build In Amsterdam

Build In Amsterdam Website

Build In Amsterdam is a branding company. Their entire site is built around the rule of thirds. The home page of this site is a simple example showing the screen divided into three sections or three images. The line of focus is drawn to the intersections of the 3x3 grid that the rule of thirds implements.
